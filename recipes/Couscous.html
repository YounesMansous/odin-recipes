Couscous Recipe

Description

Couscous is a small, granular pasta made from semolina wheat or durum wheat flour. It has a light and fluffy texture when cooked and serves as a versatile base for various dishes. With its mild and nutty flavor, couscous can be easily seasoned and customized to complement a wide range of cuisines. It is quick to prepare and can be enjoyed as a side dish or transformed into a main course by adding vegetables, herbs, and spices. Couscous is celebrated for its adaptability and has become popular worldwide.

Ingredients :

  • 11/2 cups couscous
  • 11/2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/2 cup toasted pine nuts (optional)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving (optional)

Steps

  1. In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable or chicken broth to a boil. Remove from heat and stir in the couscous. Cover the saucepan and let it sit for about 5 minutes or until the couscous has absorbed the liquid.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic, ground cumin, ground coriander, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Cook for an additional minute, stirring constantly to toast the spices.
  3. Fluff the cooked couscous with a fork and transfer it to the skillet with the seasoned onions. Mix well to combine, ensuring the couscous is evenly coated with the spices. Cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Remove the skillet from heat. Stir in the chopped parsley and mint. If desired, add toasted pine nuts for extra texture and flavor.
  5. Serve the couscous warm as a side dish or as a bed for roasted or grilled vegetables, meat, or fish. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the couscous for a refreshing tang, if desired.
